## ğŸ‘¥ Scaling: The "Neutral Partner" Rule
*Required for 2 or 3 players.*

In low-player games, not enough actions trigger naturally.
1.  Assign numbers to the actions: (1) Build, (2) Landscape, (3) Populate, (4) Sponsor.
2.  After players reveal their cards, roll a **d6**.
    *   **1â€“4:** The rolled action triggers as if a "Neutral Player" selected it.
    *   **5â€“6:** Re-roll.
3.  **Resolution:**
    *   If you **did not** pick this card, take the **Follower Action** for the rolled number.
    *   If you **did** pick this card, nothing extra happens (you already take the Leader action).

## ğŸ† Scoring
The game ends when a player has filled **10 Animals** OR the **Appeal Track** reaches 50. Complete the current round, then score.

### 1. Positive Points (+)
*   **Animals:** Sum of Appeal (VP) for all checked animals.
*   **Kiosks:** **2 VP** per Kiosk placed on the map.
*   **Coin Reserves:** **1 VP** per 3 Coins remaining.

### 2. Penalties (-)
*   **Unused Landscape:** **-1 VP** for every Terrain symbol (ğŸŒ²,ğŸª¨,ğŸ’§,ğŸŒ¾) that is **not** inside a populated enclosure. (This punishes over-preparing and failing to fill the cages).
*   **Orphan Fences:** **-1 VP** for every fence line that is not part of a completed, closed loop. (Clean up your construction site!)

**Tiebreaker:** Most Cash remaining.

*   **Kiosk Blocking:** Since Kiosks must be outside, they are excellent for "filling gaps" between cages to maximize space, but be carefulâ€”if you surround an area with Kiosks, you might not be able to run a fence line through them later.
